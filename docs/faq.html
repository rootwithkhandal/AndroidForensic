Frequently Asked Questions & Investigator Guide

Expert answers on non-rooted acquisition methodologies, cryptographic chain of custody, and forensic best practices.

📱 Core Acquisition & Root vs Non-Root Constraints
How does the framework extract data from a non-rooted Android device? ▼
On non-rooted devices, Android security isolates private application databases (`/data/data/...`). The MVP does not bypass these restrictions. Where Android permits it, the tool can query exposed content providers and collect diagnostic output; availability varies by device and release.
  • Querying public and system **Content Providers** (`content://call_log/calls`, `content://sms`, `content://com.android.contacts/data/phones`, `content://com.android.calendar/`).
  • Executing deep diagnostic system dumps (`dumpsys location --noredact`, `dumpsys notification --noredact`, `dumpsys usagestats`, `dumpsys wifi`, `dumpsys usb`).
  • Parsing system kernel logs and event buffers (`logcat -b events -d`) and active socket states (`netstat -ntupa / ss`).
What happens if the Android device has root access (`su`) enabled? ▼
Some acquisition paths may use `su` when it is already available and authorized on the device. Root access does not guarantee that every database is accessible or unencrypted, and it does not make this MVP a full filesystem or physical-acquisition tool.
💬 WhatsApp & Instant Messaging Extraction
Why are Gmail and ProtonMail folders empty when inspecting `/sdcard/Android/data/`? ▼
Modern mail applications (`com.google.android.gm`, `ch.protonmail.android`) do not store sensitive email bodies or attachments on external SD card storage (`/sdcard/`). They store encrypted SQLite databases strictly inside `/data/data//databases/`. For non-rooted acquisitions, investigators extract cloud backups or capture active notification text via our `--notifications` module.
How does the WhatsApp Companion Web sync extraction work? ▼
By leveraging WhatsApp's multi-device companion feature, investigators can link our framework's `whatsapp_companion.py` module as a linked web browser session via QR code. Once authorized, our engine syncs full historical message archives, contact JID profiles, and shared location pins directly to `evidence/CASE-*/parsed/whatsapp_companion_raw.json` without requiring root access on the phone.
🔒 Chain of Custody & Cryptographic Verification
How does the framework guarantee cryptographic integrity for court verification? ▼
Registered evidence files are recorded with SHA-256 and MD5 values, timestamps, paths, and source metadata in the case folder. These manifests support traceability; they do not by themselves guarantee legal chain of custody or forensic validity.
Can I import external Google Takeout or synced databases into an active case? ▼
Yes! If you acquire a user's Google Takeout (`Timeline.json`, `Chrome History.json`) or PC-synced `Places.sqlite` file, simply drop it into your case's `evidence/` directory. When you run `--journey` or `--browser-history`, our engines automatically scan, ingest, and merge those external records right alongside the live device evidence.
